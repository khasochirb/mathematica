Introduction to Polynomials

Polynomials are algebraic expressions consisting of variables and coefficients combined using addition, subtraction, and multiplication (but not division by variables). They are fundamental to algebra and appear in countless applications across mathematics.

Key Concepts

  • Terms: Parts of a polynomial separated by + or - signs
  • Coefficient: The numerical factor of a term
  • Degree: The highest power of the variable in a polynomial
  • Monomials: Polynomials with only one term
  • Binomials: Polynomials with two terms
  • Trinomials: Polynomials with three terms
Example: Identifying Components of a Polynomial

Consider the polynomial: 3x4 - 2x3 + 5x2 - 7x + 1

Terms: 3x4, -2x3, 5x2, -7x, 1

Coefficients: 3, -2, 5, -7, 1

Degree: 4 (highest power of x)

Leading term: 3x4

Leading coefficient: 3

Constant term: 1

Polynomial Operations

Understanding how to perform operations on polynomials is essential for algebraic manipulation.

Addition and Subtraction

To add or subtract polynomials, combine like terms (terms with the same variable and exponent).

(3x2 + 2x - 5) + (x2 - 3x + 4) = 4x2 - x - 1

Multiplication

To multiply polynomials, multiply each term of the first polynomial by each term of the second polynomial, then combine like terms.

Example: Multiplying Binomials

Multiply: (x + 3)(x - 2)

Using the FOIL method:

First terms: x × x = x2

Outer terms: x × (-2) = -2x

Inner terms: 3 × x = 3x

Last terms: 3 × (-2) = -6

Combine like terms: x2 - 2x + 3x - 6 = x2 + x - 6

Factorization Techniques

Factorization is the process of expressing a polynomial as a product of simpler polynomials. This is essentially the reverse of multiplication.

Common Factorization Methods

  • Greatest Common Factor (GCF): Factor out the highest common factor from all terms
  • Factoring Trinomials: For quadratics of the form ax2 + bx + c
  • Difference of Squares: a2 - b2 = (a + b)(a - b)
  • Sum/Difference of Cubes:
    • a3 + b3 = (a + b)(a2 - ab + b2)
    • a3 - b3 = (a - b)(a2 + ab + b2)
Example: Factoring a Trinomial

Factor: x2 + 5x + 6

Looking for two numbers that multiply to 6 and add to 5:

2 × 3 = 6 and 2 + 3 = 5

Therefore: x2 + 5x + 6 = (x + 2)(x + 3)

Applications and Further Study

Polynomials and factorization are foundational skills that appear throughout mathematics:

  • Solving polynomial equations
  • Simplifying rational expressions
  • Analyzing function behavior
  • Applications in calculus (especially integration)
  • Number theory and cryptography

Example: Application in Traditional Architecture

A Mongolian architect is designing a traditional ger (yurt) with a circular base. The area of the base is given by the polynomial πr2, where r is the radius. If the architect wants the area to be 50 square meters, what should the radius be?

Setting up the equation: πr2 = 50

r2 = 50/π

r = √(50/π) ≈ 3.99 meters

The ger should have a radius of approximately 3.99 meters to achieve the desired floor area of 50 square meters.
